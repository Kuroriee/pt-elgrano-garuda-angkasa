Our Story

We don't export coffee. We export the story of Sumatran soil.

“Every bean carries the memory of volcanic earth, highland mist, and the hands that harvested it.”

PT Elgrano Garuda Angkasa was founded on a simple conviction: the world's finest coffee grows in Sumatra's highlands — it simply needed a bridge to the world's best roasters.

Over two decades, we've built direct relationships with smallholder farmers across Aceh, North Tapanuli, and West Sumatra, creating a traceable supply chain that benefits every hand it touches.

Today, we supply specialty roasters, importers, and trading houses across Europe, North America, Asia-Pacific, and the Middle East — not as a commodity supplier, but as a long-term production partner.

From Farm to Shipment

The Production Journey

01

Cultivation

Shade-grown at 1,200–1,600m altitude. Volcanic soil, year-round rainfall. Partnered with certified smallholders using GAP standards.

02

Selective Harvest

Only red-ripe cherries hand-picked during peak season. No strip harvesting. Multiple passes per tree ensure grade consistency.

03

Wet Processing

Cherries pulped within 12 hours of harvest. Fermentation tanks control flavor development for clean, complex cup profiles.

04

Wet Hulling

Giling Basah — Sumatra's signature process. Hulling at high moisture creates the distinctive full-body, low-acid character.

05

Sun Drying

Beans dried on raised patios to 11–12% moisture. Hand-raked for even drying and the deep, earthy Sumatran character.

06

Sorting & Grading

Density tables, screen sizing, and hand-sorting remove defects. Q-Graders cup every lot to certify Grade 1 specialty quality.

07

Bagging & Storage

Vacuum-packed or GrainPro-lined jute bags in climate-controlled warehouses to preserve freshness until shipment.

08

Export & Shipment

FOB and CIF terms to 30+ markets. Full documentation, traceability, and container loading from the Port of Belawan.

Gayo Arabica

Syrupy body with notes of dark chocolate, brown sugar, and a herbaceous, cedar-like finish. Low acidity, clean aftertaste.

Flavor Profile

Gayo Arabica delivers a thick, syrupy cup with deep brown-sugar sweetness, layers of dark chocolate, and warm spice. Its acidity is low and gentle, closing with a long, soothing herbaceous, cedar-like finish — the signature of Gayo highland coffee sought after by specialty roasters worldwide.

Process & Origin

Shade-grown at 1,400m in Gayo's volcanic soil. Selectively picked at peak ripeness, then wet-hulled using Sumatra's signature Giling Basah method that gives it a full body. Certified SCA 84+ in cupping by our Q-Graders, with full traceability down to the farmer's village.

Brewing Recommendation

Ideal for pour-over and French press where its body and sweetness shine. Also excellent as a single-origin espresso base for rich crema and a long chocolate finish.

Grade 1

North Tapanuli

Mandheling

The classic Sumatran profile — full-bodied, earthy and spicy with tobacco and dark cocoa. Bold and lingering.

Wet-Hulled1,200mSCA 83+

Flavor Profile

Mandheling is the definition of classic Sumatran coffee: bold, full-bodied, and earthy. Notes of tobacco, dark cocoa, and earthy spice combine with very low acidity to produce a heavy, lingering cup with an aftertaste that clings to the palate.

Process & Origin

Sourced from smallholder farmers in North Tapanuli at 1,200m. Wet-hulled at high moisture — the technique that creates its hallmark low-acid, full-bodied character. Every lot is cupped to certify SCA 83+ quality.

Brewing Recommendation

Strongest in immersion methods like French press and tubruk that emphasize its body. A top pick for dark espresso blends that need depth and intensity.
